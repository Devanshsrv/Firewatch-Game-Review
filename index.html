Firewatch: A Game Review

Overview

Firewatch is a first-person adventure game developed by Campo Santo and released in February 2016. The game is set in the Wyoming wilderness during the summer of 1989. It is available on multiple platforms, including Windows, macOS, Linux, PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and Nintendo Switch. The game falls under the narrative-driven exploration genre, emphasizing story and emotional engagement over action. Players assume the role of Henry, a fire lookout, whose primary connection to the outside world is Delilah, his supervisor, communicated through a walkie-talkie. The game’s stunning visuals, compelling story, and unique setting have earned it widespread acclaim.

Map of Firewatch

The paper map in Firewatch is a vital navigational tool that reflects the handcrafted detail of the game’s world. It marks trails, landmarks, and key locations like Thorofare Lookout, Jonesy Lake, and Cottonwood Creek, helping players orient themselves in the expansive wilderness. The map evolves as the player explores, with Henry marking new trails, points of interest, and notes that provide context for the unfolding story. Unlike typical minimaps, this paper map requires active engagement; players must physically pull it out, align it with their compass, and interpret its features to navigate. This interaction deepens immersion, making the act of finding your way feel authentic and integral to the game’s theme of solitude and exploration.

Market

Performance and Reception Firewatch was both a critical and commercial success, selling over 2.5 million copies by 2019. It received accolades for its storytelling, voice acting, and visual design, including nominations at The Game Awards and BAFTA. Awards 2016 BAFTA Games Awards: Won "Best Performer" for Cissy Jones as Delilah. SXSW Gaming Awards: Won "Excellence in Visual Achievement." Game Developers Choice Awards: Nominated for "Best Narrative" and "Best Debut." The Game Awards 2016: Nominated for "Best Independent Game" and "Best Art Direction." Competitors The game’s closest competitors include titles like Gone Home and What Remains of Edith Finch, which also focus on narrative-driven exploration. However, Firewatch stands out due to its dialogue mechanics and outdoor setting.

Possible improvements

Incorporating more dynamic wildlife interactions or environmental puzzles could deepen the immersion. While the dialogue choices offer some variation, adding more branching storylines or alternate endings could encourage replay. A multiplayer or co-op mode, allowing players to take on the roles of Henry and Delilah in real-time, could offer a fresh perspective on the story. Technical enhancements, such as improved rope physics for more complex climbing sequences and additional weather effects like rain, could elevate realism and engagement.

Personal Insights

Playing Firewatch is a meditative experience that underscores the importance of human connection and the impact of solitude. Its narrative depth has influenced my understanding of storytelling in interactive media, showing how minimalism and emotional resonance can create lasting impressions.
